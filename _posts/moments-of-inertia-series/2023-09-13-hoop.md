Some also call this a "zero-thickness torus".

## Parametarizing the Surface

We can do this with just standard cylindrical coordinates

$$
\b{r}(\phi, z) = R \; \u{s} + z \; \u{z}
$$

Therefore

$$
dA = R \; d\phi \; dz
$$

<br>

We use cylindrical coordinates to evaluate this integral. Since it is a cylindrical shell, the parameter $s = R$ is constant while the parameters $\phi$ and $z$ vary. Therefore, $dA = R \; d \phi \; d z$.

$$
\begin{align}
    M &= \int dm \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \int dA \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \int_{-L/2}^{L/2} \; R \; d\phi \; dz \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma R \left ( \int_{0}^{2 \pi} d \phi \right ) \left ( \int_{-L/2}^{L/2} dz \right ) \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma R \left ( 2 \pi \right ) \left ( L \right ) \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \cdot 2 \pi R L
\end{align}
$$

$$
\begin{align}
    I &= \int r_{axis}^2 \;dm \\[10pt]
    &= \int R^2 \;dm \\[10pt]
    &= R^2 \int dm \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \cdot 2 \pi R^3 w \\[10pt]
    &= M R^2
\end{align}
$$

<br>

Without loss of generality, we can assume the axis of rotation lies on the $x$-axis. As an exercise, evaluate the integral if the axis of rotation is on the $y$-axis. 

$$
\begin{align}
    I &= \int r_{axis}^2 \;dm \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \int r_{axis}^2 \;dA \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{-L/2}^{L/2} r_{axis}^2 \;s \; d\phi \; dz \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{-L/2}^{L/2} (R \ \sin \phi)^2 \;R \; d\phi \; dz \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma R^3 \left ( \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sin^2 \phi \; d\phi \right ) \left ( \int_{-L/2}^{L/2} dz \right ) \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma R^3 \left ( \pi \right ) \left ( L \right ) \\[10pt]
    &= \sigma \cdot \pi R^3 L \\[10pt]
    &= \frac{1}{2} M R^2
\end{align}
$$

## Inertia Tensor of a Hoop

$$
I = \begin{bmatrix}
    \frac{1}{2} M R^2 & 0 & 0 \\
    0  & \frac{1}{2} M R^2 & 0 \\
    0  & 0 & M R^2
\end{bmatrix}
$$
